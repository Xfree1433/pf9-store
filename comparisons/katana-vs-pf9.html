Katana MRP vs PF9 Manufacturing Suite

Katana is a polished MRP platform built for makers selling on Shopify and similar channels. PF9 covers different ground — shop-floor operations beyond inventory and production. Here's how they compare.

Choose Katana if
  • • You sell on Shopify and need real-time inventory sync
  • • Production planning and BOM workflows are core
  • • Native Xero/QuickBooks integration matters
  • • Your team is small (1–3 users) and Starter tier fits
Choose PF9 if
  • • You have a shop floor with shifts, inspections, and quality forms
  • • 6+ users on the system (Katana tier breaks bite hard)
  • • You want flat pricing with no per-user surprises
  • • Shop-floor operations matter more than e-commerce sync

What Katana does well

Honest sections aren't a sales tactic.

Native Shopify, Xero, and QuickBooks integration

Katana syncs inventory, orders, and accounting in real time across e-commerce and books. For a maker selling on Shopify with QuickBooks for accounting, Katana eliminates double-entry across three systems. PF9 exports to CSV and to QuickBooks Online format; native real-time sync isn't in product today.

Production scheduling and BOM management

Katana has a strong BOM hierarchy, manufacturing orders, and a production scheduler that sequences work across resources. PF9 doesn't have BOM workflows or production scheduling; FLOWTRACK tracks raw material → WIP → finished goods but doesn't plan it.

Modern UI built for makers

Katana's interface is one of the better-designed manufacturing tools — clean, fast, mobile-friendly. The product was built for craft and contract manufacturers who graduated from spreadsheets. PF9's UI is similarly clean but optimized for shop-floor data entry rather than maker-style production runs.

Where PF9 wins

Flat pricing as your team grows

Katana's tier breaks (Starter→Standard at 2 users, Standard→Pro at 5–10 users) plus per-user fees compound quickly. At 10 users you're at ~$799/mo. PF9 stays at $249/mo from 1 user to 250.

Shop-floor operations beyond MRP

SHIFTLOG (shift handoffs), QUALIFI (NCR/CAPA), and INSPECTR (field inspections) are operational tooling Katana doesn't ship. If your day-to-day pain is "what happened on second shift" or "how do I track NCRs," PF9 is built for that.

Faster deploy

Katana onboarding requires BOM cleanup, inventory taking, and integration setup. PF9's apps run in days. SHIFTLOG works the first day a supervisor logs a handoff; FLOWTRACK works as soon as you import an item list.

14-day money-back guarantee

Subscribe, run real shifts, full refund within two weeks if it's not the fit. Katana offers a 14-day free trial; PF9's model is a paid trial with refund.

Migrating from Katana to PF9

  1. Export from Katana: items, vendors, customers, and inventory levels as CSV.
  2. Import items + inventory to FLOWTRACK. BOMs are not a PF9 concept — workflow change.
  3. Set up SHIFTLOG, QUALIFI, INSPECTR from scratch — these aren't Katana features.
  4. Reconfigure Shopify sync: if you used Katana's native sync, you'd switch to a CSV-based or webhook approach with FLOWTRACK.
  5. Cancel Katana at end of billing period.

Honest note: if Shopify-Xero-QuickBooks real-time sync is core to your operation, PF9 is not a drop-in replacement. Many shops keep Katana for production and add PF9 for shop-floor operations — they don't have to be either/or.
