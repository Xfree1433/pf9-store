Fishbowl Inventory vs PF9 Manufacturing Suite

Fishbowl is a long-running inventory and manufacturing platform with deep QuickBooks integration. PF9 takes a flat-rate, browser-based approach. Here's where each fits.

Choose Fishbowl if
  • • Real-time QuickBooks bidirectional sync is non-negotiable
  • • You're comfortable with desktop installation and IT setup
  • • You need deep manufacturing-edition features (work-order BOM)
  • • Your team is small enough that per-user fees don't dominate
Choose PF9 if
  • • You want browser-based, no installation, no IT setup
  • • Flat pricing matters more than real-time QB sync
  • • You have 6+ users and don't want per-seat fees
  • • Shop-floor operations (shifts, quality, inspections) matter

What Fishbowl does well

Honest sections aren't a sales tactic.

Deep QuickBooks integration

Fishbowl was originally built as a QuickBooks-adjacent inventory manager and has the most mature QuickBooks integration in the manufacturing space. Real-time bidirectional sync, including item-level cost flow. If your bookkeeper insists on QuickBooks-as-source-of-truth, Fishbowl is purpose-built for that. PF9 exports to QuickBooks Online format but isn't bidirectional.

Manufacturing Edition with work orders and BOMs

Fishbowl Manufacturing Edition adds multi-level BOMs, work orders, and production tracking on top of the inventory base. For shops that need real BOM management as part of inventory, Fishbowl ships that natively. PF9 doesn't have BOMs.

Long track record

Fishbowl has been in market since 2001 and is a known quantity in the SMB manufacturing space. Implementation partners, third-party support, and integrations are abundant. PF9 is much newer; ecosystem is thinner.

Where PF9 wins

Flat pricing as your team grows

Fishbowl's per-user fees compound. At 25 users you're at $1,300+/mo before add-ons. PF9 stays at $249/mo. The math is dramatic above ~6 users.

Browser-based, no install

Fishbowl historically required Windows desktop installation; cloud version exists but the implementation pattern still leans on IT involvement. PF9 is browser-only — works on any laptop, tablet, or shop-floor terminal without IT setup.

Shop-floor operations beyond inventory

SHIFTLOG, QUALIFI, and INSPECTR cover shift handoffs, quality workflows, and inspections that Fishbowl doesn't ship as native modules. If your day-to-day pain isn't inventory but rather "what happened on second shift," PF9 is the better tool.

Faster deploy

Fishbowl Manufacturing implementations typically take weeks for BOM cleanup, item data migration, and QuickBooks sync configuration. PF9 deploys in days; SHIFTLOG works the first day.

Migrating from Fishbowl to PF9

  1. Export from Fishbowl: items, customers, vendors, and inventory levels as CSV.
  2. Import items + inventory to FLOWTRACK. BOMs and work orders don't carry over — those are not PF9 concepts.
  3. Set up SHIFTLOG, QUALIFI, INSPECTR from scratch.
  4. Configure QuickBooks export from FLOWTRACK to replace Fishbowl's real-time sync. CSV-based instead of real-time.
  5. Cancel Fishbowl at end of license term (annual contracts may apply).

Honest note: if real-time bidirectional QuickBooks sync is core to your operation, PF9 is not a drop-in replacement. CSV export to QuickBooks Online format is the closest equivalent. For shops where shift management and quality matter more than QB sync, PF9 is a clear win.
