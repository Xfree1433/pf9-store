# Demo Script — Manufacturing Suite

**Audience:** ICP-1 Plant Manager / Ops Director (primary) and ICP-2 VP Ops (for OPSIQ).
**Length:** 20 minutes, hard stop at 25. If you need longer, you haven't qualified.
**Goal of the call:** one of three outcomes — (1) same-day subscription via storefront, (2) 7-day trial commitment with a scheduled check-in, (3) clean disqualification.
**Never leave a demo with "I'll send some info."**

---

## Pre-call prep (10 minutes before)

- [ ] Pull up their company website — know what they make
- [ ] LinkedIn of the attendee — confirm title, tenure
- [ ] Scan any reply history in CRM for stated pain
- [ ] Have two storefront demo tabs open: SHIFTLOG and FLOWTRACK (the lead apps)
- [ ] Have pricing page and bundle page ready
- [ ] Confirm CRM is open and you're ready to take notes

If the prospect is <20 employees or >500 employees: reschedule as "let me put you with a better-fit person" and route accordingly. Don't waste the slot.

---

## 0:00–2:00 — Open

> "Thanks for the 20 minutes, {{first_name}}. Before I share my screen — quick agenda: I'll ask you 3 or 4 questions so I don't demo the wrong thing, then I'll show you exactly the pieces that matter for {{company}}, and we'll leave 5 minutes at the end for price and next step. Sound good?"

Wait for yes.

> "And one more — what made you take this call? I want to make sure we hit what you were hoping to see."

**Shut up and write down the answer verbatim.** This is the entire demo — everything that follows serves this sentence.

---

## 2:00–7:00 — Discovery (5 minutes, max 4 questions)

Pick 3–4 from this list. Don't survey — dig on the first answer that lands.

1. "How are you tracking shift handoffs today — paper, spreadsheet, whiteboard, or something else?"
2. "When an incident happens on second shift, how does the Monday morning team find out?"
3. "Inventory counts — frequency, and what happens between counts?"
4. "Quality — are you running paper inspection forms or digital?"
5. "Have you looked at software for this before? What stopped you?" *(almost always: price, per-user fees, or "it was going to take 6 months")*
6. "Who else in the shop touches this problem?"

**Landing the disqualifier:** if they say "we have a full ERP we love" or "we're integrating with {{SAP/Oracle/Epicor}} next quarter" — stop. Say: "Sounds like you're solved. Not sure we're the right fit for {{company}} today. Mind if I send a one-pager so you have us on file?" End the call at minute 8. You just saved both of you 12 minutes.

---

## 7:00–17:00 — Demo (10 minutes)

**Rule:** show two apps, not five. The two that match the pain they named in minute 2. If they named three pains, show two and say "the third one is covered by {{app}} — I'll send the live demo link after this."

### Flow per app (5 minutes each)

1. **Before/after frame** (30 sec) — "Right now your second-shift supervisor writes on a clipboard. Here's what that looks like in SHIFTLOG."
2. **Live demo, not slides** (3 min) — use the storefront demo; click, type, show it actually works. Never say "imagine if…"
3. **The one surprising thing** (1 min) — e.g., "notice this took 12 seconds, no training, and it's already searchable by any supervisor tomorrow."
4. **Confirm it lands** (30 sec) — "Does that map to how you'd want to run it?" Wait for the answer.

### Things not to do

- Don't explain the admin panel unless asked
- Don't show integrations unless asked
- Don't mention the roadmap. Ever.
- Don't say "enterprise-grade"
- Don't open the pricing page until the close

---

## 17:00–20:00 — Price + Close

> "Here's what it would look like for {{company}}."

Share screen on the pricing page. Say the numbers out loud.

> "{{App1}} is ${{price1}}/month flat. {{App2}} is ${{price2}}. Together that's ${{total}}/month, no per-user charge, no setup fee. Most shops start there and add FLOWTRACK once shifts are running clean. Or you grab the full five-app bundle for $249/month and you're covered for anything that comes up this year."

**Then shut up.**

First person who talks loses. Let them respond. Options:

### They say yes

> "Great. Two ways to do this — you can subscribe right now on the storefront and I'll stay on the line while you do it, or I'll email the link and you can do it tonight. Which is easier?"

Stay on the line if they pick the first option. Walk them through Stripe checkout. Confirm the welcome email arrives before you hang up.

### They say "let me think about it"

> "Totally fair. Before I let you go — what's the specific thing you want to think about? Price, the apps, timing, or something about the demo itself?"

Handle the actual objection. See §Objection handling below.

### They say "I need to talk to {{boss/partner/team}}"

> "Makes sense. Two things — one, do you want me on that call, or do you want a short doc you can forward? Two, can we put a 15-minute follow-up on the calendar for {{day 5 or 6 out}} so it doesn't get lost?"

Always book the follow-up on the live call. Never "I'll send times."

### They say no

> "Appreciate the honesty. One quick question — was it the fit, the pricing, or something else? Helps me not waste your time later if something changes."

Write down the real reason. Mark in CRM. Move on.

## Objection handling

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "Too expensive" | "Compared to what? If it's compared to Excel, you're right, Excel's free. If it's compared to {{their last quote}}, we're usually 80% cheaper. Which one is the benchmark for you?" |
| "We need [feature we don't have]" | "We don't do that today. Want me to be honest about whether we're on the roadmap or not? {{Yes/no answer, no fluff.}} If that's a must-have, we're not the right fit this quarter." |
| "I need to check with IT" | "Totally. It's a web app, no install, runs on any browser — IT usually signs off in a day. Want me to send a one-pager with the security summary so that conversation is 5 minutes?" |
| "Send me some info" | "Happy to — but info sits in an inbox. Would you rather I send the info, or book 15 minutes next week where we pick up exactly here? Your call." |
| "We're too small / too big" | If too small: "Got it. The live demo is free forever, so poke around when you're ready." If too big: "Fair — we cap well below enterprise. I'll close this out." |
| "Does it integrate with {{X}}?" | Honest answer. If no: "Not today. You'd export CSV. If native integration is a must-have, we're not the fit." Don't oversell roadmap. |

## Quality bar

A demo is well-run if all of these are true:
- [ ] You talked less than 60% of the call
- [ ] They said a number out loud (employee count, units, shifts, something)
- [ ] They saw the product work, not a slide
- [ ] You left with a specific next step on the calendar, not "I'll send info"
- [ ] CRM is updated before you take the next call

If any of those fail, shadow the recording with the PF9 founder before your next demo.
